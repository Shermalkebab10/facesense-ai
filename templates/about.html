The Story Behind FaceSense AI

The idea for FaceSense AI came from Sheema's observation during the COVID-19 pandemic — in crowded public spaces, manually monitoring mask compliance was slow, inconsistent, and unreliable. She asked herself a simple question: what if a camera could do this automatically, and tell you not just whether someone is wearing a mask, but also how they are feeling?

"I wanted to build something that wasn't just a class exercise — something that could genuinely be used in hospitals, schools, or public spaces to make people safer and more aware."

That vision became FaceSense AI. Starting from raw datasets and building two separate deep learning models from scratch, Sheema designed the entire pipeline — from data preprocessing and model training to a polished, real-time web application with live camera support.

What makes this project stand out is not just the technology, but the thinking behind it — combining two distinct AI tasks (mask detection and emotion recognition) into a single, seamless experience that works in real time on any device with a camera.
